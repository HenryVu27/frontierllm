## Orientation {#orientation}

Alibaba's Qwen team is the most prolific open-weights frontier-LLM lab as of May 2026 by release cadence. Across Qwen2 (June 2024), Qwen2.5 (September 2024), and Qwen3 (April 2025), the team shipped over a dozen variants — base models, instruction-tuned chat models, code specialists, math specialists, vision-language models, and a reasoning-specialised QwQ line — under permissive licensing. The technical contributions are less architecturally novel than DeepSeek's, but the *release discipline* (frequent, well-documented, with detailed model cards) made the Qwen line a primary substrate for the academic open-weights community.

This chapter walks the Qwen family. Calibration: May 15, 2026. Cross-references to earlier modules are dense; Qwen's contribution is in *execution* rather than in *new techniques*, so the chapter is comparatively short.

**Qwen2 (June 2024).** 0.5B / 1.5B / 7B / 57B-MoE / 72B variants. The 57B-MoE configuration uses 14B activated parameters. Dense models use GQA, RoPE, SwiGLU, RMSNorm — the standard 2024 stack. Trained on ~7T tokens with ~30 language coverage. MMLU on the 72B variant reached ~84.2%, competitive with Llama 3 70B at the time.

**Qwen2.5 (September 2024).** 0.5B / 1.5B / 3B / 7B / 14B / 32B / 72B variants. The major changes: 18T training tokens (versus Qwen2's 7T), expanded context (32K standard, 1M token long-context variant), improved instruction-tuning and multilingual coverage. The 72B variant pushed MMLU into the high-80% range. The Qwen2.5 family was the production workhorse line across late 2024 and early 2025 — the academic and on-device community's preferred mid-scale model set.

**Qwen3 (April 2025).** 0.6B / 1.7B / 4B / 8B / 14B / 32B / 235B-A22B / 480B-A35B variants. The two large variants are MoE (235B total with 22B active; 480B total with 35B active). Training scale: 36T tokens, 119 language coverage. The headline feature is the **hybrid reasoning/non-reasoning toggle** (next section). Qwen3-235B-A22B sits around DeepSeek-V3 territory on knowledge benchmarks; Qwen3-480B-A35B is the lab's largest open-weights release to date.

The release cadence is the structural feature: roughly six months between major versions, with point-version refreshes (Qwen2.5-1.5B, Qwen2.5-Math, Qwen2.5-Coder, Qwen3-Embedding, etc.) at ~2-month intervals.

## Qwen3 hybrid reasoning {#qwen3-hybrid-reasoning}

Qwen3 introduced an explicit **reasoning / non-reasoning mode toggle** at inference time. A boolean flag (or a special token) tells the model whether to engage long chain-of-thought reasoning. With reasoning on, the model emits a chain of thought and is calibrated for reasoning-heavy benchmarks (AIME, GPQA, code). With reasoning off, the model emits short responses and is calibrated for low-latency chat use.

**Why a toggle.** The trade-off Module 5 § 05a and the GPT-5.2 mode design (§ 06a) both name: reasoning is expensive at inference time, and not every query benefits. Qwen3 is the most explicit open-weights instantiation of the trade-off — a single weight set, two inference behaviours, user-controllable.

**Implementation hints.** Qwen3's hybrid mode is not documented in detail. Public evidence suggests it is trained via a curriculum that mixes reasoning-style and non-reasoning-style targets, with the mode signal acting as a condition on the response distribution. The architecture is the standard MoE Transformer; only the post-training is mode-aware.

**Thinking budget.** Qwen3 also exposes a *thinking budget* parameter — the maximum length of the reasoning chain. The mechanism is the explicit version of the implicit budget OpenAI's o-series exposes via the o3-mini low/medium/high tiers.

The toggle is the practical adaptation to a key tension across 2025–2026: production users want reasoning when it helps but pay-for-it latency and cost when it doesn't.

## Vision and math variants {#vision-and-math-variants}

**Qwen-VL line.** Qwen-VL → Qwen2-VL → Qwen2.5-VL → Qwen3-VL. The Qwen-VL line adds a vision encoder atop the corresponding Qwen base model. Qwen2.5-VL introduced **dynamic FPS sampling** for video (Module 0g for the tokenization side) — frame rate adapts to video content rather than being fixed — and **temporal grounding** evaluations. Qwen3-VL has reportedly matched proprietary multimodal models on document understanding benchmarks (Chinese-language and English-language) at smaller scale.

**Qwen2.5-Math.** A math-specialised variant of Qwen2.5 with tool-integrated reasoning (TIR): the model can emit a code-execution token to run a computation step before continuing the chain of thought. The TIR pattern is the same primitive Module 3 § 03h covers under tool-use post-training. Qwen2.5-Math reaches AIME-2024 ~35% at the 72B scale with TIR enabled, far surpassing the base Qwen2.5 72B's ~16%.

**QwQ-32B.** A reasoning-specialised model trained from a Qwen2.5-32B base with outcome-reward RL (Module 3 § 03f). The release demonstrated that a 32B model with outcome RL could match DeepSeek-R1 (671B base) on several reasoning benchmarks — providing a smaller-footprint, open-weights alternative to R1 for academic and on-device reasoning. QwQ-32B is one of the most-cited open-weights reasoning models of 2025.

## QwQ line {#qwq-line}

The **QwQ (Qwen-with-Questions) line** is the Qwen team's reasoning specialisation track:

- **QwQ-32B (November 2024).** The original. 32B parameters; reasoning RL on a Qwen2.5-32B base. Headline numbers: AIME-2024 ~50%, MATH-500 ~90%, both competitive with DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B and substantially above QwQ's non-reasoning Qwen2.5 base.
- **QwQ variants** continued into 2025 with Qwen3-Thinking — the reasoning-mode-enabled flagship Qwen3 — effectively absorbing the QwQ line into the Qwen3 mainline with the hybrid toggle.

The QwQ → Qwen3-Thinking arc is the Qwen team's lineage decision: rather than maintain a separate reasoning model family long-term, integrate reasoning into the main line with a runtime toggle. Compare DeepSeek's V3 / R1 separation (still distinct lines as of V3.2) and OpenAI's GPT-5 absorption of o-series (GPT-5.2 mode toggle, § 06a).

## Post-training recipe {#post-training-recipe}

The Qwen post-training pipeline is documented at greater detail than most frontier labs; the lab's adoption of **Tülu-3-style** training is explicit in the Qwen3 technical materials. The four-stage pipeline:

1. **SFT.** Large supervised mixture combining helpfulness, code, math, and tool-use demonstrations.
2. **DPO.** Direct Preference Optimization on a preference dataset. Module 3 § 03b for the algorithm.
3. **RLVR.** Verifier-reward RL on math and code tasks where ground truth is available. Module 3 § 03e.
4. **Multi-objective RL.** Combining helpfulness, harmlessness, instruction-following rewards.

The pipeline is recognisably the same as Meta's Llama 3.1 iterative-DPO recipe (§ 06d), AI2's Tülu 3 (the published reference), and DeepSeek-R1's four-stage R1 recipe (§ 06e). The convergence of post-training recipes across labs is one of the dominant themes of the 2025–2026 frontier-LLM landscape — Module 3 covers this in detail.

## Open-weights cadence {#open-weights-cadence}

Qwen ships under the Apache 2.0 license for most variants (some larger variants under a Qwen-specific license that is broadly Apache-compatible). The release cadence in 2024–2026:

| Date | Release | Variants |
|---|---|---|
| Jun 2024 | Qwen2 | 0.5B/1.5B/7B/57B-MoE/72B |
| Sep 2024 | Qwen2.5 | 0.5B/1.5B/3B/7B/14B/32B/72B base + Instruct |
| Sep 2024 | Qwen2.5-Coder | 1.5B/7B/32B |
| Nov 2024 | Qwen2.5-Math, QwQ-32B | math + reasoning specialists |
| Jan 2025 | Qwen2.5-VL | vision-language refresh |
| Apr 2025 | Qwen3 | 0.6B–480B-A35B |
| 2025+ | Qwen3-VL, Qwen3-Embedding, point-version refreshes | continuous |

Five major releases plus a dozen specialised variants in 12 months. Comparison: Meta released Llama 3.0, 3.1, 3.2, 3.3 across April–December 2024 (four releases) and Llama 4 in April 2025 (one major release in 2025). DeepSeek released V2 (May 2024), V3 (December 2024), R1 (January 2025), V3.2 (October 2025) — four major releases across 18 months. Qwen's cadence is faster than either, optimised for open-weights ecosystem velocity.

## Summary {#summary}

Alibaba's Qwen line is the open-weights frontier release with the fastest cadence (5+ major versions plus a dozen specialised variants in 12 months), the widest multilingual coverage (~119 languages in Qwen3), and the most explicit production-oriented design choices (hybrid reasoning toggle, thinking budgets, TIR for math). Architecturally, Qwen is conservative — standard MoE Transformer with the now-canonical GQA / RoPE / SwiGLU / RMSNorm stack — and trains at scales comparable to DeepSeek and Llama 4 (235B–480B MoE flagships). The team's contribution to the broader frontier-LLM field is *operational*: a sustained, fast-cadence open-weights release programme with detailed model cards and a comprehensive specialist-variant ecosystem.

For a reader of earlier modules: Qwen's architecture ↔ Module 0 throughout; the hybrid-reasoning toggle ↔ Module 3 § 03f; the four-stage post-training pipeline ↔ Module 3 §§ 03a–03e; Qwen2.5-Math's TIR ↔ Module 3 § 03h. The Qwen line is one of the cleanest substrates on which to study modern open-weights frontier-LLM practice end-to-end.

Two empirical anchors as of May 15 2026: Qwen3-480B-A35B (the production flagship, frontier-tier on knowledge and reasoning benchmarks) and Qwen3-32B with reasoning mode enabled (a small-footprint reasoning model widely used in academic interpretability work). Open questions: full training-data composition (Qwen has not published a full Llama-3.1-style technical report), the exact hybrid-toggle training procedure, and the relationship between Qwen3-Thinking and the discontinued QwQ line.
