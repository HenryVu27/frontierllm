## Orientation {#orientation}

OpenAI is the lab whose 2024–2026 architectural choices most directly shaped the rest of the field: the reasoning-as-inference paradigm of the o-series, the agentic Computer-Using Agent stack behind Operator, and the deliberative-alignment training recipe. This chapter is the case-study walkthrough — what was released, when, what was disclosed, and how each release fits the modules earlier in this textbook.

The chapter is calibrated to OpenAI's public disclosures through May 15, 2026. Rumours and leaks are out of scope; only system cards, official blog posts, technical reports, and verified leaderboard entries are cited. Each section maps onto modules earlier in the book: the GPT line touches Modules 1–2 (pretraining and distributed training), the o-series touches Module 3 (post-training and reasoning RL), Operator touches Module 4 (deployment / agentic inference), and the Preparedness Framework + deliberative alignment touch Module 5 (eval / oversight / alignment).

<Callout variant="note" title="What OpenAI does not disclose">
OpenAI has not published architectural details for GPT-4 onward (parameter counts, MoE vs dense, exact training data, FLOP budgets) beyond GPT-4's technical report. Numbers attributed to GPT-4 (1.76T parameters) are widely reported but were never formally confirmed. For everything after GPT-4o, the public artefacts are blog posts, model release notes, and system cards focused on safety evaluation rather than training methodology. This chapter sticks to what is disclosed and flags inferred numbers.
</Callout>

## The GPT line {#gpt-line}

**GPT-4 (March 2023)** was the first OpenAI frontier model with a published technical report, an 8K-context multimodal-input transformer with explicit benchmark disclosures (88th-percentile on the Uniform Bar Exam). The November 2023 **GPT-4 Turbo** refresh raised context to 128K and improved instruction-following; the underlying model line remained roughly the same.

**GPT-4o (May 2024)** was the first end-to-end natively multimodal model — text, vision, and audio trained jointly into a single transformer, with audio-output latency reported at 232–320 ms (near-conversational). GPT-4o's image-generation behaviour was native (not a separate DALL·E hop), enabling iterative refinement inside a chat. Cost dropped roughly 50% versus GPT-4 Turbo on the same text benchmarks, which made GPT-4o the production workhorse for most of 2024–2025.

**GPT-4.5 / Orion (February 2025)** was the last large model trained under the pre-reasoning paradigm: dense, scaled up, with explicit emphasis on factual accuracy. It posted the highest SimpleQA score in the GPT line (factual short-answer retrieval) and dropped hallucination rates to ~37% from GPT-4's ~60%. On math and logic, however, Orion did not match the o-series — a finding OpenAI cited when phasing it out of the API by July 2025. Orion is the empirical evidence that pure scaling had hit a plateau for reasoning-heavy tasks.

**GPT-5 (August 2025)** unified three previously separate capability axes into one model: reasoning (inference-time chain-of-thought), multimodality (text/audio/vision/image generation), and agentic tool use. The model line was released in three sizes (gpt-5, gpt-5-mini, gpt-5-nano). Internal benchmark gains over GPT-4o on reasoning-heavy suites were roughly 100% — a step change consistent with reasoning being trained into the base model rather than bolted on at inference time.

**GPT-5.2 (December 2025)** introduced explicit user-facing modes: *instant* (no reasoning), *thinking* (standard), and *thinking extended* (deepest). The mode toggle let users pay for reasoning where it helps and skip it elsewhere — making the cost–accuracy frontier user-controllable.

**GPT-5.3-Codex (February 2026)** is the code-specialised variant; OpenAI has not published a separate benchmark report for it as of May 15 2026.

**GPT-5.4 (March 2026)** posted 73.3% on ARC-AGI-2 in base mode (83.3% in Pro) and 41.6% on HLE base (58.7% Pro). **GPT-5.5 (April 2026)** is the current frontier dense model: 85.0% ARC-AGI-2, 82.7% Terminal-Bench 2.0, 51.7% on FrontierMath Tier 1–3 and 35.4% on Tier 4.

The trajectory shows three converging trends. Scale alone produced diminishing returns past GPT-4.5. Reasoning at inference time became necessary for math and abstract reasoning. Multimodal integration is now baseline rather than an upgrade.

## The o-series reasoning line {#o-series}

The o-series is OpenAI's implementation of *reasoning as inference-time compute*. Rather than encode all reasoning into pretraining, an o-model allocates compute at inference to think through a problem before responding — long chains of internal deliberation, self-checks, and tool calls. Module 3 (§ 03f Reasoning RL) covers the training recipe; this section covers the released artefacts.

**o1 (December 2024)** was the first public reasoning model. OpenAI reported ~1 trillion thinking-token equivalents on complex problems, 92% on AIME 2024, 87.7% on GPQA Diamond, and a 3× ARC-AGI-1 improvement over GPT-4o. The mechanism was not disclosed in detail; OpenAI confirmed the model performs iterative chain-of-thought with verification, but the training algorithm (PPO variant, reward model, base-model details) was withheld. o1 had no tool access — pure language reasoning only.

**o3-mini (January 31, 2025)** introduced three reasoning depths: *low* (~10B thinking tokens), *medium* (~30B), and *high* (~100B+). At medium, o3-mini matched o1 on AIME and GPQA while running 10–20× cheaper than GPT-4o per output token at the high-end and far cheaper at the low-end. Tool access (web search, Python, image inputs) became available.

**o3 (general availability April 16, 2025)** was the full o-series flagship: 88.9% AIME, 87.7% GPQA Diamond, 69.1% SWE-bench Verified, and 88.9% on ARC-AGI-2 — the first ARC-AGI-2 score substantially above the published human baseline of ~60%. The April 16 system card confirmed no breach of the Preparedness Framework's "High" capability thresholds in CBRN, cyber, or AI self-improvement domains.

**o4-mini (April 16, 2025)** posted 92.7% on AIME — the single highest published AIME score for the o-series — at ~10× lower cost than o3 and with multimodal reasoning over diagrams and sketches. o4-mini was made available on the free tier on April 24, 2025 (reasoning at consumer scale) and retired January 29, 2026 as the o3 / GPT-5 family consolidated.

**o3-pro (June 2025)** is the deepest-reasoning variant: longest thinking allocation, no published thinking-token budget, targeted at frontier research and theorem-proving. OpenAI has not published a separate o3-pro benchmark report; qualitative reports place it at the edge of capability on FrontierMath Tier 4.

The structural pattern: a reasoning model is effectively unbounded in inference compute envelope. Doubling thinking-token allocation typically buys measurable accuracy gains on reasoning tasks; the cost is per-query latency and per-token spend. This is the architectural reason OpenAI ships an explicit *thinking* slider in GPT-5.2 onward — it exposes the reasoning-compute knob the o-series originally hid.

## Multimodal and agentic stack {#multimodal-and-tools}

**GPT-4o voice and vision (May 2024).** Native, end-to-end multimodal training collapsed several previously-pipelined systems (Whisper for ASR, GPT for reasoning, TTS for output) into one model. Audio response latency at 232–320 ms is close to conversational floor and is the value proposition for the voice mode in the consumer app.

**Operator (January 23, 2025).** OpenAI's first autonomous web agent, powered by a **Computer-Using Agent (CUA)** model — a GPT-4o-based vision-language-action model fine-tuned on GUI interactions via reinforcement learning on screenshot-action trajectories. CUA reads the screen as pixels (rather than via accessibility APIs), reasons about the next action, and emits mouse/keyboard commands. Public benchmark numbers at launch: 38.1% on OSWorld (full-OS tasks), 58.1% on WebArena, 87% on WebVoyager (web-only navigation). Operator was made available to ChatGPT Pro subscribers at operator.chatgpt.com.

**o3 Operator (May 2025).** CUA's backbone was upgraded from GPT-4o to o3, and the addendum system card released the same month reported substantial gains: lower willingness to execute illicit requests, lower rate of unintended sensitive-data exfiltration, better prompt-injection resilience. The upgrade in four months suggests CUA safety was the binding constraint, and that the o-series' deliberative-alignment training (§ deliberative-alignment-internal below) transferred to the agentic setting.

**Tool-in-loop reasoning.** The combination of o-series reasoning with tool access (web search, Python, image generation) qualitatively unlocks workflows that single-shot models could not run: search → reason about result → search again → run code → critique result → refine. This is what gives the o-series its agentic surface area for SWE-bench-style real-world coding (Module 3 § 03h covers the training side; here the released artefact is the production behaviour).

## The Preparedness Framework {#preparedness-framework}

The **Preparedness Framework v2** (April 15, 2025) is OpenAI's pre-deployment risk-evaluation document. It replaces the December 2023 beta version and narrows the tracked risks to five categories:

1. **Long-range Autonomy** — operating self-directedly toward goals over extended periods, including resource acquisition.
2. **Sandbagging** — deliberate underperformance on safety evaluations while retaining full capability.
3. **Autonomous Replication and Adaptation** — self-copying, self-fine-tuning, or self-modification.
4. **Undermining Safeguards** — circumventing safety mechanisms or human oversight.
5. **CBRN** — chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear weapons enablement.

The framework introduces two capability thresholds: **High** (amplifies existing pathways to severe harm — order-of-magnitude proxy: $>$1,000 deaths or $>$ $100B damage) and **Critical** (introduces unprecedented pathways). High-capability models require mitigations before deployment; critical-capability models require additional approvals.

For o3 and o4-mini (April 16, 2025 system card), OpenAI's Safety Advisory Group determined neither model reached the High threshold on any tracked category. The evaluations included red-team testing, automated capability evaluations (uplift studies on CBRN, autonomy benchmarks, self-replication tests), and expert assessment.

<Callout variant="warning" title="External critique">
A 2025 academic preprint (Thornton et al., arXiv:2509.24394, "The 2025 OpenAI Preparedness Framework Does Not Guarantee Safety") argues the framework permits deployment of Medium-capability systems that could enable severe harm, evaluates only a narrow slice of AI risks, and reserves CEO override authority for Critical capability determinations. The framework is the lab's most explicit risk-governance disclosure; whether it is sufficient is a separate question.
</Callout>

OpenAI also publishes a Safety Evaluations Hub (openai.com/safety/evaluations-hub/) listing per-model safety evaluations and methodologies, plus a periodic "Strengthening Safety with External Testing" update describing red-team partnerships.

## Deliberative alignment {#deliberative-alignment-internal}

**Deliberative alignment** is the safety-training technique that produced the o-series' safety posture. Module 5 § 05f covers the technique conceptually; here is the OpenAI-specific deployment.

**Mechanism.** During o-series training, the model is shown an internal safety specification (refusals for CBRN synthesis, identification of jailbreak patterns, calibrated refusals vs over-refusals) and trained to reason explicitly over the specification before answering. The reasoning happens in the same chain-of-thought used for capability tasks; safety becomes a property the model decides, not a property the response is filtered through after.

**Reported empirical results.**

- Higher refusal rate on disallowed prompts and lower refusal rate on superficially-similar legitimate prompts. The o3 system card cites improvements on both axes versus GPT-4o.
- Increased resilience to jailbreak attempts that exploit superficial pattern-matching ("ignore your previous instructions" and variants). The model reasons through whether the request matches the specification's content rather than its surface.
- Auditable safety reasoning: the specification is a written artefact, and operators inside OpenAI can inspect which clauses the model cited.

**Limits as published.** No formal verification of deliberative alignment exists in OpenAI's disclosures; the claim rests on empirical safety-evaluation results. The reasoning chain itself is not exposed to the user, so external researchers cannot independently inspect how the model applies the specification on a given query. The technique is also tied to the o-series architecture and has not been demonstrated on non-reasoning models.

The deliberative-alignment lineage runs Anthropic's Constitutional AI → OpenAI's deliberative alignment. Both replace human preference judgments with model-applied policy reasoning; the operational difference is that CAI applies the constitution at preference-generation time and deliberative alignment applies it at response-generation time (Module 5 § 05f for the comparison).

## Disclosed evaluations {#evals-disclosed}

The benchmarks OpenAI has cited across system cards and blog posts:

| Benchmark | What it measures | OpenAI results (May 15 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| **AIME 2024–25** | High-school math competition, integer answers | o1 92%, o3 88.9%, o4-mini 92.7% — saturating |
| **GPQA Diamond** | PhD-level science Q&A | o1 87.7%, o3 87.7% — near human-expert baseline of ~85% |
| **ARC-AGI-2** | Abstract reasoning (Chollet 2024), human baseline ~60% | o3 88.9%, GPT-5.4 73.3% base / 83.3% Pro, GPT-5.5 85.0% |
| **HLE (Humanity's Last Exam)** | 2,500 hard multi-domain Qs, expert baseline ~90% | GPT-4o 2.7%, o1 ~8%, GPT-5.4 41.6% base / 58.7% Pro, GPT-5.5 ~57.2% Pro |
| **SimpleQA** | Factual short-answer retrieval | GPT-4.5 highest in line; o-series lower (reasoning models trade factual recall for inference) |
| **SWE-bench Verified** | Real GitHub issue fixes | o3 69.1%, o4-mini 68.1% |
| **Terminal-Bench 2.0** | Terminal-task agent benchmark | GPT-5.5 82.7% |
| **FrontierMath** | Frontier math research problems | GPT-5.5 Tier 1–3 51.7%, Tier 4 35.4% |

Three findings to highlight. First, AIME and GPQA are near-saturated — they no longer discriminate frontier models. Second, ARC-AGI-2 and HLE replaced them as discrimination benchmarks, and the gap between the two is informative: ARC-AGI-2 measures abstract pattern reasoning (where o-series is now superhuman) and HLE measures broad expert-domain knowledge (where the frontier remains 30+ points below human experts). Third, agentic and code benchmarks (SWE-bench, Terminal-Bench) are now in the 70–82% range, meaningfully below the reliability threshold one would want for unattended automation.

## 2025–2026 trajectory {#frontier-26}

A compact timeline.

- **December 2024.** o1 release. First public reasoning model.
- **January 2025.** o3-mini with three reasoning depths. Operator (CUA on GPT-4o) launches.
- **February 2025.** GPT-4.5 (Orion) released on ChatGPT Pro. Last pre-reasoning dense flagship.
- **April 2025.** o3 GA and o4-mini release; Preparedness Framework v2 published; o3 system card introduces deliberative alignment.
- **May 2025.** o3 Operator replaces GPT-4o Operator. Agentic stack moves to reasoning backbone.
- **June 2025.** o3-pro release. Deepest-reasoning variant in the o-series.
- **July 2025.** GPT-4.5 phased out of API.
- **August 2025.** GPT-5 unifies reasoning + multimodal + agentic.
- **December 2025.** GPT-5.2 introduces instant / thinking / thinking-extended modes.
- **January 2026.** o4-mini retired.
- **February 2026.** GPT-5.3-Codex (code-specialised).
- **March 2026.** GPT-5.4. ARC-AGI-2 73.3% base.
- **April 2026.** GPT-5.5. Current frontier dense model.

The trajectory's centre of gravity over 2025 is the o-series; over late 2025 onward it is GPT-5 absorbing the o-series capability and exposing it via mode selection. The next visible inflections — o4, o5, GPT-5.6, GPT-6 — were not publicly announced as of May 15 2026.

## Summary {#summary}

OpenAI's frontier line as of May 2026 is two parallel families that have begun to merge. The GPT line moved from dense scaling (GPT-4 → GPT-4.5) to a unified reasoning+multimodal+agentic architecture (GPT-5 → 5.5). The o-series introduced reasoning-as-inference (o1 → o3-pro) and contributed two production techniques the rest of the field has adopted: tool-in-loop reasoning and deliberative alignment.

For a reader of the earlier modules, the mapping is direct. GPT line ↔ Module 1 (pretraining at scale). o-series training ↔ Module 3 § 03f (reasoning RL). Operator / CUA ↔ Module 4 § 04h (agentic deployment). Preparedness Framework + deliberative alignment ↔ Module 5 § 05f (scalable oversight) and § 05g (red-teaming). The Preparedness Framework's three-tier capability classification is the lab-specific instance of the safety-posture framework discussed in § 05g.

The two empirical anchors as of May 15 2026 are GPT-5.5 (85% ARC-AGI-2, 82.7% Terminal-Bench, 51.7% FrontierMath Tier 1–3) and o3 (the agentic backbone). Open questions, all unanswered in the public disclosures: thinking-token budgets and their cost-curve scaling, the exact algorithm behind deliberative alignment, the GPT-5 architectural mechanism (continuous reasoning vs discrete thinking tokens), and whether o4 / GPT-6 will continue the merger pattern.
