## About this module {#about}

Modules 0–5 covered the *methods* of frontier-LLM construction: architecture, pretraining, distributed training, post-training, inference and serving, evaluation, alignment, interpretability. This module covers the *artefacts*: a comparative survey of the 2024–2026 frontier-model families. Each chapter is the "what they actually did" deep dive for one lab or lab cluster.

After the eight chapters, a reader should be able to take any frontier-lab technical report or tech blog and locate every engineering decision in the taxonomy of Modules 1–5. DeepSeek-V3's auxiliary-loss-free MoE routing? Module 0h. Llama 3.1's 405B parallelism stack? Module 2 § 02h. OpenAI's deliberative alignment? Module 5 § 05f. Anthropic's Computer Use? Module 4 § 04h. Gemini's iRoPE? Module 0c. Qwen3's hybrid reasoning toggle? Module 3 § 03f. The chapters here are case-study walkthroughs at the lab level rather than method-level reintroductions; nothing is re-derived.

<Callout variant="note" title="Calibration and scope">
Content is calibrated to public lab disclosures through May 15, 2026. Each chapter sticks to what the lab has actually disclosed in tech reports, system cards, model cards, peer-reviewed papers, official blog posts, or verified leaderboard entries. Rumours, leaks, and unverified third-party claims are out of scope. Where a number is hedged or inferred, this is flagged in the chapter text.
</Callout>

<Callout variant="note" title="A note on iteration cadence">
One cross-cutting observation about lab structure deserves naming before the per-lab chapters. The cleanest open disclosure of how a *small focused team* iterates on a frontier-style recipe is the Hugging Face SmolLM3 playbook (Allal et al., 2025), which documents a two-to-three-person pretraining team running on a roughly quarterly recipe-iteration cadence — the BLOOM → StarCoder → StarCoder2 → SmolLM → SmolLM2 → SmolLM3 trajectory. The thesis the playbook makes explicit is that every recipe-improvement cycle compounds, so labs that train quarterly accumulate advantages relative to labs that train once per year, at fixed total compute. The closed-frontier labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, the closed Chinese frontier) are presumed to operate at substantially larger team scale, but their iteration cadence is not publicly disclosed. The contrast is not closed-vs-open; it is *cadence-disclosed-vs-not-disclosed*, and the per-lab chapters that follow inherit that disclosure asymmetry — Hugging Face's recipe is fully visible, most others are visible only as model artefacts and tech reports.
</Callout>

## A frontier-lab snapshot (May 15, 2026) {#snapshot}

For quick orientation, the comparative state across the major labs:

| Lab | Current flagship | Architecture | Open weights? | Disclosure depth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OpenAI | GPT-5.5, o3-pro | Unified dense + reasoning | No | System cards, no tech reports |
| Anthropic | Claude Opus 4.7, Mythos (restricted) | Closed; presumed dense | No | Detailed safety; no architecture |
| Google DeepMind | Gemini 3.1 Pro, Gemma 4 31B | Native multimodal MoE | Gemma only (Apache 2.0) | Mixed; Gemini 1.5 report detailed |
| Meta | Llama 3.1 405B; Llama 4 family | Dense (3.x), MoE (4.x) | Yes (Meta Community License) | Most detailed (Llama 3.1 Herd report) |
| DeepSeek | DeepSeek-V3.2, R1 | MLA + MoE + DualPipe + DSA | Yes (MIT-style) | Most architectural detail |
| Qwen | Qwen3-480B-A35B | MoE; hybrid reasoning | Yes (Apache 2.0) | Detailed model cards + tech reports |
| Mistral | Large 3, Magistral | MoE flagship, dense reasoning | Partial (Small, Magistral Small) | Modest |
| xAI | Grok-4.3 | Proprietary | Only Grok-1 (historical) | Minimal |
| Cohere | Command-A | Proprietary, agentic-tuned | Partial (Command-R 35B) | Minimal |
| AI21 | Jamba 1.5 (52B) | SSM-Transformer hybrid | Yes (Apache 2.0) | Detailed |
| Moonshot | Kimi K2.5 (1.04T-MoE) | MLA + MoE | Partial | Modest |
| Zhipu | GLM-4.5 (355B-MoE) | MoE + tool-use | Partial | Detailed |
| MiniMax | M1 (456B/45.9B) | Lightning Attention + MoE | Partial | Detailed |

Three high-level trends visible in this table:

1. **MoE has won at frontier scale.** Every lab whose architecture is disclosed has shipped MoE flagships in 2025–2026 (OpenAI's exact GPT-5 architecture remains undisclosed, but the cost/throughput profile is consistent with MoE). The DeepSeek / Gemini / Qwen / Llama 4 lines all converged on the same architectural family.
2. **Reasoning lines bifurcated, then re-converged.** o1 / R1 / QwQ / Magistral were initially separate reasoning model families; by mid-2025 the production trend was reasoning-mode toggles on a single weight set (GPT-5.2, Qwen3-Thinking, Doubao Seed 2.0's slow-thinking mode).
3. **Open vs closed is bimodal.** Western frontier (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) is closed; Western mid-frontier (Meta, Mistral, AI21, Cohere) ships partial open weights; Chinese frontier (DeepSeek, Qwen, Moonshot, Zhipu, MiniMax) is the most open-weights-prolific cluster. The open-weights frontier is increasingly Asian-led.

## What success looks like {#success-criteria}

After completing the module a reader should be able to:

1. Read a frontier-lab tech report and locate every method-level decision in the Module 0–5 taxonomy.
2. Identify which architectural innovations originated where (MLA → DeepSeek-V2; iRoPE → Gemini 1.5; auxiliary-loss-free MoE → DeepSeek-V3; deliberative alignment → OpenAI o3; Computer Use → Anthropic Claude 3.5 → o3 Operator).
3. Describe the safety-governance landscape across labs (OpenAI Preparedness Framework v2, Anthropic RSP v3.2, DeepMind FSF v3.0) and compare their threshold structures.
4. Compare the lineage of post-training pipelines across labs and identify the recurring four-stage SFT → DPO → RLVR → multi-objective pattern.
5. Place a frontier-lab release in the comparative landscape — knowing which benchmark numbers are credible, which are unverified, and which benchmarks are saturated.
